Digitializing My Past Works

When I moved back home for my last semester of university, I was sentimental and felt a little cheated that my senior year will have to be spent at home. While cleaning my stuff, I found a box of past works from high school. I picked three to redraw digitially and hopefully add a layer of depth that 16 year old me did not have at the time when I conceieved these drawings. A common theme across these drawings is fragmentation of self and sense of interpersonal reflection. Artistically, all three peices have emphasis, which is a element used in art to draw attention to a certain part of the work. In “Baby Puzzle” and “Taped Self”, the emphasis came from color, whereas in the last drawing, emphasis is created from use of space. Looking back at the orginal hard copies of these drawings, I could tell you exactly what I was thinking and feeling at the time when I drew these, but that’s a story for another day.
