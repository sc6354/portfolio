Project 6 : Tableau Dashboard of My Spotify Data

May 20, 20205 | 2 minutes read
Share this: Twitter Facebook Pinterest

Tags: Tableau, Data Visualization, Jupyter

Project Overview

This project started from a desire to uncover more pattens in my music streaming data that Spotify does not present in their annual Wrapped Up. Essentially I wanted to explore how my streaming times and the music that I listened to changed with events in my life and the pandemic. From pure observation of using Spotify between my laptop and phone, I did noticed that I mostly listened to songs that were released two-three years back during this past year. To help me further investigate this, I built a Tableau Dashboard with a range of visualizations.

Timeline of data: May 2020 – May 2021.
Tools used: Tableau, Jupyter Notebook

Final Dashboard

Part 1: Data Extraction and Processing

To obtain your Spotify data, you need to request it. After a couple weeks of waiting for Spotify to sent me my data, I finally received an email to download my data from my account. The data was packaged in json files. I used Jupyter notebook and Pandas to read the json files, convert “msPlayed” (Stands for how many mili-seconds the track was listened) to minutes played. There is nothing wrong with mili-seconds but minutes was a bit easier to wrap my head around and compare streaming times, so I changed it.

Part 2: Creating the Views

This is my first time using Tableau for data visualization. Previously I would either visualize data in a Jupyter Notebook, R, or create web visualizations with D3 and React. Naturally, I didn’t find the software difficult to use.

Conclusion

This project is a milestone in my data visualization journey. I was able to synthesize two different datasets and weave them into a cohesive personal story. I enjoyed every step in the process! I used all kinds of different tools for this project, and this was for sure a step for me to gain more technical skills. I learned to use Tableau and create simple interactivity using Adobe XD. In terms of gaining some insights to my relationship with Spotify, I was surprised. I have always been a fan of pop music, but data told me that I actually listened to sleeping aid music more than anything else! Overall, not only did I learned to deal with datasets and turn them into beautiful visualizations with various tool, I also learned more about myself and what role Spotify plays in my daily life.
